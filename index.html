ODISHA DESIGN WEEK:

CULTURALLY CONNECTED

The brainchild of the Odisha Design Council (ODC), Odisha Design Week (ODW), aims to bridge the gap between the rich cultural heritage of the state with modern design practices from around the world by conducting progressive design events and activities promoting innovative design techniques that augment sustainable economic development of the state, which in turn leads to the growth of the national economy.

ODW will be the meeting ground where design lovers can interact with and learn from an array of design professionals invited from across the globe through workshops, panel discussions, heritage tours, exhibitions and much more. Odisha Design Week (ODW) aspires to be an amalgamation of traditional culture and contemporary design.


11 - 15 DECEMBER 2021

The Theme of ODW

The theme of this edition of ODW is “Culturally Connected,” - which will essentially focus on the practicality of design thinking within businesses by implementing key strategies and techniques to utilize local resources and skills to their maximum capacities.

Why is this theme line - “Culturally Connected”?

Odisha is known for its overwhelmed rich Culture with traditional design practices. On the other hand, the state disconnected from the modern design revolution regarding awareness and education. The contemporary Design Community has not yet discovered the design practices, philosophies that are part of ancient Odisha. In its first edition, the Odisha Design Week (ODW) aims to bridge the gap between Modern Design Practices and the Cultural tradition of Odisha in every possible means to enrich design methodologies and technological domains on both sides. Hence, let’s get culturally connected!

Under the umbrella of saying “ Culturally Connected,” the areas precisely we are focusing in considering the post-Covid economy:

  1. Integrating Design into urban spaces
  2. Fusion between Heritage and Smart city movements
  3. System design in the health sector
  4. Design thinking in skill development (Aligned with Mining Industry)
  5. Design against natural calamity - Cyclone (Ongoing project)
  6. Operational excellence by service design

DESIGNER'S DESTINATIONS

There are several favorite places in and around the smart-heritage city Bhubaneswar for designers to explore and chill out.

KONARK

About 70 kms from the city of Bhubaneswar, Konark is the town where the language of stone surpasses the language of man. The greatest attraction remains to be the Sun Temple, which is the summit of Odisha Temple Architecture and an UNESCO World Heritage Site. It continues to leave its visitors spellbound with its massive structure, precision and intricate detailing.

PURI-KONARK MARINE DRIVE

An enthralling drive through the Chandrabhaga Beach and coastal forest running parallel to each other. It offers breathtaking views on either sides and compels one to take a break!

lingraj

Situated in the city of Bhubaneswar, a masterpiece of Indian Architecture for its elaborative plan, proportions, elegant craftsmanship and impressive dimensions, the Lingaraj temple is the symbol of unification of Odia Culture.

mukteswar

Built during the 10th Century AD, Mukteswar Temple is situated in Bhubaneswar. It is referred as a ‘miniature gem’ by historians for its intricate precision and craftsmanship. The carvings of Mukteswar are filled with intricately carved fineries.

raja rani

Built during the 11th Century AD, the Rajarani Temple is situated in the heart of Bhubaneswar and is dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is another proud architecture of the Odisha culture for its fine detailing and exquisite craftsmanship and is managed by the Archaeological Survey of India (ASI).

kalabhumi craft museum

A breathtaking museum in Bhubaneswar that celebrates the craftsmanship of local artisans. It has two blocks – a display area of handicrafts & handlooms and a live section with an amphitheater and workshop zones.

tribal museum

It is located in Bhubaneswar and provides insight into the tribal culture. It displays the different stages of tribal evolution, their attire, ornaments and food habits. The museum library too, is a great source of information for learning about the tribal traditions.

mukteswar

A craft village in the bustling city of Bhubaneswar is an initiative led by the Odisha State Government, that promotes not just local handicrafts and textiles but also handicrafts from all over India. Visitors also have the opportunity to relish a wide variety of local cuisine.

utkalika

It is the Odisha State Handicraft Corporation set up in the year 1959 to provide marketing assistance to handicraft artisans. It also conducts skill training and product development programmes besides organizing exhibitions.

ratnagiri

It is a well excavated Buddhist Monastery, 97 kms from Bhubaneswar, and the only one of its kind in the country to have curvilinear roof. Its numerous Stupas and well ornated carved rooms set in serene surroundings on top of a hillock, depict the greatness of that period.

balakati

A craft village in the bustling city of Bhubaneswar is an initiative led by the Odisha State Government, that promotes not just local handicrafts and textiles but also handicrafts from all over India. Visitors also have the opportunity to relish a wide variety of local cuisine.
